WHAT

What are Deepfakes ?

Deepfake is a term used to describe a type of artificial intelligence-based technology that is used to generate synthetic media, such as images, videos, and audio. This technology uses machine learning algorithms to manipulate and generate new content that is designed to look and sound like real people or events.

WHEN

First Appearance

Deepfake technology first appeared in November 2017 when an anonymous user on the social media platform Reddit posted an algorithm that leveraged existing artificial intelligence algorithms to create realistic fake videos

HOW

How are they created ?

Deepfakes are created using a machine learning technique called generative adversarial networks (GANs) . A GAN consists of two neural networks: a generator and a discriminator. The generator creates synthetic images, videos or audio, while the discriminator tries to distinguish the synthetic content from real content. During the training process, the generator and discriminator compete with each other and gradually improve, until the generator produces content that is virtually indistinguishable from the real thing. To create a deepfake, a large dataset of real images, videos or audio of a target person is used to train the generator to mimic their appearance, voice, or mannerisms. The output of the generator is then combined with a source video or image to replace the face, voice or body movements of the original person in the source.
